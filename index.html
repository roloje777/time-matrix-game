Game Rules & Instructions

How to Play

  1. Read the Activity: An activity description will appear above the Time Matrix.
  2. Classify the Activity: Click on the quadrant (Q1, Q2, Q3, or Q4) where you think this activity belongs.
  3. Get Feedback: You'll see immediate feedback - green for correct, red for incorrect.
  4. Learn: If incorrect, you'll see which quadrant was correct with an explanation.
  5. Continue: The next activity will appear automatically after a short delay.

The Four Quadrants

Q1: Important & Urgent

Examples: Crises, deadlines, emergencies, urgent problems

Strategy: Handle immediately, but try to reduce these through better planning

Q2: Important & Not Urgent

Examples: Planning, prevention, values, relationship building

Strategy: Schedule time for these - they're key to long-term success

Q3: Not Important & Urgent

Examples: Some calls, meetings, interruptions, some emails

Strategy: Delegate or minimize - they feel urgent but aren't truly important

Q4: Not Important & Not Urgent

Examples: Time wasters, excessive entertainment, trivial busywork

Strategy: Eliminate or minimize - these are pure time drains

Scoring

  • +1 point for each correct classification
  • 0 points for incorrect answers (no penalty)
  • See your progress and final accuracy percentage

Learning Goals

  • Identify what's truly important vs. just urgent
  • Recognize time-wasting activities
  • Focus energy on Q2 activities for long-term success
  • Reduce time spent in Q1 through better planning

Pro Tip:

The goal isn't just to win the game, but to learn how to apply these principles to your real life. Ask yourself: 'Which quadrant does this activity in my life belong to?'
